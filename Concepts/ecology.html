Ecology also involves interactions with organisms we can't see. Micro-organisms play an important role on planet Earth. This quiz answer demonstrates knowledge of their importance on a small scale.

Q: Explain the significance of bacteria in the nitrogen cycle, including why you need them in a fish (or shrimp) tank.

A: Although nitrogen makes up most of the air we breath, mammals do not posses the mechanisms to ingest it through respiration. Instead, bacteria in the air and ground must first perform the process of nitrogen fixation. Afterward, nitrogen conversion is performed through another set of bacteria. Only then is the nitrogen a suitable form to be used by plants and then other organisms who eat those plants. Bacteria are necessary in a fish tank because otherwise a fish or shrimp's food would not have any usable nitrogen.
