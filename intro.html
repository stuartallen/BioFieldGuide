A Field Guide

By Stuart Allen for Environmental Biology (BI 101) at OSU

This field guide contains contextualized information that I have learned from the OSU Environmental Biology Class (BI 101). This guide demonstrates the three aspects of science knowledge: concepts, skills and connections. The goal of science education is to create scientifically literate people. This course has improved my scientific literacy in the biological field through learning about biodiversity, ecology and conservation biology. I am now able to present it through images, writings, and sketches as well use different media to teach it.

I will continue to add to this field guide in the future as a way to display my life long learning. There are many things on my science learning bucketlist that I can upload here for all to see. One of them includes the largest organisim in the world, a fungus in the Malheur National Forest. I want to explore, describe and explain much more of the world around me. The learning life cycle never ends!
