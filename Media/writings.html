To demonstrate my writing media abilities I decided to display and talk through my safety checklist. A wall of text like this is intimidating to a new science-learner even if they should know all of the information here. That is why an audio file can be paired with it so the learner feels less intimidated and more engaged.

Stuart Allen · Safety Checklist

Indoor Activities

  • Practice good hygiene by washing all materials after use during an experiment
  • Never store anything taken from outside the laboratory in the same storage as food or drink
  • Wash hands before and after any indoor experiment
  • Only remove an organism from its’ natural environment if necessary. If possible afterwards, return it to where it came from
  • When possible, place things on cutting board rather than directly on the counter
  • Have the indoor safety kit ready, organized and restocked before any experiment

Outdoor Activities

  • Wash hands before and after any excursion
  • Bring and apply hand sanitizer as needed
  • Have the outdoor safety kit ready, organized and restocked before any experiment
  • Have the location of the outdoor safety kit be known and nearby at all times

Personal

  • Location
    • Make sure any and all roommates and guests are aware of any experiment
    • In addition, make sure anyone in the house during an experiment is aware of the hazards and risks
  • Equipment
    • Keep the fire extinguisher handy during any experiment
    • Do not place electronics near water or in/around the sink

Habits

  • Wash hands and apply sanitizer liberally before, during, and after experimentation
  • Avoid touching the face during experimentation

Practice

  • Leave any environment (indoor or outdoor) better than it was found
  • Clean any instrument used during an experiment. A keen eye is necessary to find all the nooks and crannies of a device that may be hard to clean
  • Never stack equipment
  • Lay out and inspect all equipment before an experiment
